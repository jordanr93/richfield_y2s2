Privacy and Data Protection

One of the biggest ethical concerns in big data is protecting individuals' privacy. Organizations
often collect vast amounts of personal data from customers, which could include sensitive
information such as health records, financial details, or browsing habits. The risk is that this data
could be misused or exposed due to breaches, leading to harm. Legally, businesses must comply
with data protection laws such as the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) in the EU, or
similar regulations in other parts of the world, which outline strict guidelines for how personal
data should be collected, stored, and processed.

For example, businesses need to obtain explicit consent from individuals before collecting their
data, and they must ensure that data is stored securely. Failure to do so can lead to legal
consequences, fines, and damage to the organization’s reputation.
