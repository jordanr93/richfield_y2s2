3.5.6 Supply Chain and Logistics

IoT improves supply chain and logistics operations by offering real-time tracking of goods,
enhancing transparency and reducing inefficiencies.




                                                                                                  46

[PDF page 55; printed page 47]
   IoT Technologies: RFID and GPS track the location of goods, while temperature and humidity
   sensors monitor conditions for sensitive products. Communication is often powered by
   LoRaWAN, cellular networks, and Wi-Fi.

   Standards: GS1 standards, including GTIN (Global Trade Item Number), help track products
   across the supply chain. ISO/IEC 18000 defines RFID standards for logistics applications.

Example: In logistics, IoT systems track the location and condition of perishable goods during
transport. Temperature sensors alert operators if conditions go out of range, while data is
securely transmitted using TLS/SSL.

Summary

IoT technologies and standards play a crucial role in a wide range of industries, from smart homes
and healthcare to agriculture and logistics. These applications benefit from the use of
communication protocols, data formats, security measures, and interoperability standards,
which ensure devices from different manufacturers can work together seamlessly. By adopting
these standards, industries can realize the full potential of IoT, improving efficiency, reducing
costs, and enhancing quality of life.
