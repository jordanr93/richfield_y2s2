Data Ownership

Another complex issue is the ownership of data. In some cases, data collected from individuals
or through third-party sources may not be clearly owned by anyone. For instance, who owns the
data when it's generated by a consumer using a service, or who has the right to use data obtained
from publicly available sources? Companies need to carefully define data ownership and usage
rights to avoid legal disputes.

Additionally, organizations should respect the rights of data creators and be mindful of how they
share or sell data to third parties. This not only has legal implications but also impacts the
company's ethical standing. Clear consent and transparency in ownership are essential in
navigating these concerns.
