Summary

IoT communication protocols are essential for ensuring devices can transmit and receive data
effectively within a network. Application communication protocols like MQTT, CoAP, and DDS
focus on lightweight, real-time data transfer, suitable for environments where bandwidth and
power are limited. On the other hand, network communication protocols such as 6LoWPAN,
Bluetooth, and LoRaWAN ensure that IoT devices can connect efficiently across various network
types, optimizing data transfer and power usage for diverse applications from smart cities to
healthcare. These protocols are foundational in enabling the seamless functioning of the IoT
ecosystem, supporting scalable, efficient, and reliable communication across devices and
networks.
