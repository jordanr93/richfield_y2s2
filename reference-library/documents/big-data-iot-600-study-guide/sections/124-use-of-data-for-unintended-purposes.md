Use of Data for Unintended Purposes

Big data can sometimes be repurposed for unintended uses, which can lead to ethical concerns.
For instance, data collected for one purpose (like improving a product) could later be used for
another (like targeted marketing). If customers were not informed that their data would be used
in this way, it could be seen as a breach of trust.

Ethically, organizations should use data only for the purposes they have communicated to users.
From a legal standpoint, businesses must ensure that they comply with the agreements they’ve
made with customers and ensure that data usage aligns with those agreement

Ethical and legal considerations in big data strategy are crucial to ensuring that organizations
operate responsibly and maintain public trust. Companies must prioritize data protection,
