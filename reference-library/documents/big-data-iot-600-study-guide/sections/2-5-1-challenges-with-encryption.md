2.5.1 Challenges with Encryption
By transforming data into unintelligible formats that require a decryption key to decode,
encryption protects the secrecy of data. Nevertheless, there are difficulties in integrating
encryption into IoT systems:
Challenges:
Limited Resources:
Strong encryption methods like AES-256 or RSA are challenging to successfully implement on
many IoT devices due to their constrained computing, memory, and energy capabilities.
The complexity of end-to-end encryption:


It can be difficult to guarantee that data is encrypted at every stage of its lifetime, from devices
to cloud storage, particularly when several protocols and devices are used.
Management of Keys:


It might be logistically challenging to securely manage and distribute encryption keys among
billions of IoT devices.
Effects of Inadequate Encryption:
listening in on conversations between cloud systems and Internet of Things devices.
Sensitive user information is made public by data breaches.
