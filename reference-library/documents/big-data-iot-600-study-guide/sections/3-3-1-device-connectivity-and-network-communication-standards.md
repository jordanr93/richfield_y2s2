3.3.1 Device Connectivity and Network Communication Standards

IoT devices require reliable, efficient, and scalable communication to ensure smooth interaction
across the network. Standards that govern this connectivity ensure that devices can operate
across multiple networks, ensuring optimal performance even in constrained environments.

IPv6 and 6LoWPAN:

These standards are designed to support IoT devices' connection to the internet, even in low-
power or constrained environments. IPv6 provides a large address space for devices, while
6LoWPAN enables IPv6 to operate efficiently in networks with low bandwidth, such as those
found in smart metering and environmental monitoring systems.

IEEE 802.15.4:

This is the foundational standard for low-power, wireless communication in IoT, often used by
protocols like Zigbee and Thread. It supports mesh networking, allowing devices to communicate
efficiently in IoT networks such as smart homes and industrial settings.

LoRaWAN and NB-IoT:

These standards are designed for long-range, low-power communication. LoRaWAN supports
communication over unlicensed radio frequencies, ideal for applications like smart agriculture
and environmental sensors. NB-IoT is a cellular technology that operates on existing LTE
networks, ideal for applications that require wide coverage with low energy consumption.
