2.6.4 Agriculture (Smart Farming)

IoT plays a critical role in modernizing agriculture through precision farming techniques. By
utilizing connected devices and sensors, farmers can monitor crops, soil, and livestock in real
time, optimizing resources and improving agricultural efficiency.

   •   Applications:

           Soil Sensors: These sensors monitor soil moisture, temperature, and nutrient levels,
           enabling farmers to optimize irrigation and fertilization, leading to better crop health
           and yield.

           Livestock Monitoring: IoT devices track the health, location, and behavior of animals,
           providing farmers with valuable insights to ensure their well-being.

           Autonomous Drones: Drones equipped with IoT sensors can survey fields, applying
           pesticides or fertilizers precisely where needed, reducing waste and environmental
           impact.
