3.1.10 Nanotechnology

Nanotechnology plays a significant role in IoT by enabling the development of ultra-small,
efficient sensors and devices. These technologies have broad applications, particularly in
healthcare, where miniaturized sensors can monitor patient health in real time, or in
environmental monitoring, where small devices can detect pollution levels or other factors at a
micro scale.

These diverse technologies come together to form the backbone of the Internet of Things,
enabling a wide range of applications across industries such as agriculture, healthcare,
transportation, and manufacturing. As IoT continues to evolve, advancements in these
technologies will further enhance the capabilities and applications of connected devices, creating
smarter and more efficient systems.
