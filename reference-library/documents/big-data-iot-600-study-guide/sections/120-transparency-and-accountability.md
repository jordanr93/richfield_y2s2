Transparency and Accountability

Another ethical issue is transparency in how data is collected and used. Many consumers are
unaware of the extent to which their data is being collected or how it’s being used to make
decisions about them. Organizations need to be open about their data practices and ensure that
people know what data is being collected and how it’s being used. This involves providing clear
privacy policies and offering customers control over their data, such as the ability to opt-out of
certain data uses.

Legally, companies must be transparent about how they use data for decision-making, especially
in sectors like healthcare or finance, where data can directly impact individuals’ lives. Failing to
disclose the purpose of data collection could lead to legal actions or government scrutiny.
