1.4 Big Data Analysis Techniques

Descriptive analysis
The goal of descriptive analysis is to identify patterns, trends, and insights by analysing and
interpreting historical data. The objective is to assist organisations comprehend past events by
converting raw data into comprehensible formats like reports, infographics, and dashboards.
Questions like "What happened?" and "What is the current state of things?" are addressed by
descriptive analysis.

Application of descriptive analysis
Business Intelligence (BI):
A historical perspective of corporate performance is provided by descriptive analysis, which is
essential to business intelligence (BI).
Healthcare:
Hospital performance, treatment results, and patient data can all be summarised with the aid of
descriptive analysis. It can display patterns such as the rise in the prevalence of a specific illness
or the typical recovery period for a given therapy.
Marketing:
Marketers examine consumer demographics, purchasing patterns, and interaction with
promotional content to determine the efficacy of campaigns using descriptive analysis.
Finance:
In order to learn more about prior performance, the financial industry uses descriptive analysis
to look at historical trends in stock prices, trading volumes, and market conditions.
