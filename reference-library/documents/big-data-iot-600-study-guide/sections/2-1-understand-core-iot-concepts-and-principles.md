2.1 Understand Core IoT Concepts and Principles

The term "Internet of Things" (IoT) describes the network of physical objects, sensors, software,
and other technologies that are connected to one another and communicate and share
information online. These gadgets, which are frequently called "smart" devices, include
everything from industrial machinery and automobiles to wearable technology and home
appliances. IoT makes it possible to gather, share, and analyse data in real time, which facilitates
automation, better decision-making, and more efficiency in a variety of fields.
According to the Internet of Things (IoT), commonplace items will be equipped with
microcontrollers, digital communication transceivers, and suitable protocol stacks so they can
communicate with one another and with clients and eventually become a vital part of the
Internet.
