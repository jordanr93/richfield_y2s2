1.2.1 Sources Of Big Data

Big Data originates from a wide variety of sources, encompassing both traditional and modern
avenues of data generation. One primary source is business transactions, where activities such
as online purchases, banking, and financial transactions generate vast amounts of structured
data. social media platforms like Facebook, Twitter, and Instagram produce an immense volume
of unstructured data in the form of posts, comments, likes, and shares, reflecting real-time
human interactions and trends.
Another significant source is the Internet of Things (IoT), where connected devices, sensors, and
wearables continuously collect and transmit data. For example, smart home devices, fitness
trackers, and industrial machinery contribute streams of data that are often time-sensitive and
diverse. Web and digital activities also play a crucial role, with search engines, e-commerce
websites, and digital advertisements tracking user behavior, preferences, and interactions.
