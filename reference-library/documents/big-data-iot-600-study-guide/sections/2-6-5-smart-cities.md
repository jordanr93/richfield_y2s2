2.6.5 Smart Cities

IoT technologies are at the heart of building smart cities, where interconnected devices and
systems optimize urban management, improve city services, and enhance the quality of life for
residents.

   Applications:

          Smart Traffic Management: Real-time data from IoT sensors allows cities to reduce
          traffic congestion, optimize traffic flow, and improve safety on roads.

          Waste Management: IoT-enabled systems optimize garbage collection by monitoring
          waste levels in bins and adjusting collection routes accordingly.

          Smart Street Lighting: Streetlights equipped with IoT sensors adjust brightness based
          on weather conditions or pedestrian activity, helping save energy and improve public
          safety.

   Benefits:

          These innovations lead to reduced energy consumption, more efficient urban
          management, and an overall improvement in the quality of life for city residents.
