1.2 Characteristics Of Big Data
Volume, Velocity, Variety, Veracity, and Value are the five basic key attributes that most
researcher and experts use to describe big data. The following is a basic description of the five
V's in big data:




Figure 1.2: The Five Vs : https://consoleflare.com/blog/big-data-solution-of-segas-game-

Volume
The enormous amount of data that overwhelms companies is referred to as volume. The days
when businesses domestically resourced and stored their data on local servers are long gone.
Terabytes of data were handled by businesses fifteen years ago. From sources including
transaction processing systems, emails, social networks, customer databases, website lead grabs,
monitoring devices, and mobile apps, data has expanded to petabytes, if not exabytes, of bytes
(1,000–1 million TB) in size today. Managers utilize data lakes, warehouses, or data management
systems to deal with all of this data. They use service providers like Google Cloud or store data
on clouds. Additionally, as the amount of data worldwide increases from two zettabytes at the
start of the decade to 181 zettabytes every day by 2025, even this may not be enough. (Leah
Zitter, 2024)
