2.6.2 Healthcare (IoT in Healthcare or IoMT)

The Internet of Medical Things (IoMT) has become an integral part of modern healthcare,
facilitating real-time monitoring, remote care, and personalized treatment plans. IoT devices in
healthcare improve the quality of care, reduce hospital visits, and support proactive health
management.

   •   Applications:

           Wearable Devices: Devices like Fitbit and glucose monitors track vital health metrics
           such as heart rate, blood sugar levels, and physical activity, allowing for continuous
           health monitoring.

           Connected Inhalers and Smart Pills: These devices help ensure medication adherence
           by tracking usage and sending reminders to patients.

           IoT-enabled Hospital Beds: These beds can monitor patient movement and vital signs,
           helping healthcare providers offer timely interventions when needed.

   Benefits:

           The widespread use of IoT in healthcare leads to better patient outcomes, fewer
           hospital visits, and more personalized care options tailored to individual needs.
